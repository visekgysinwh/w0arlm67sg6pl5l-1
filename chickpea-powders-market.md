<p><p>Chickpea powders are categorized primarily into two market types: Desi and Kabuli. Desi chickpeas, smaller and darker, are prevalent in South Asian cuisine and produce a nuttier, earthy flavor. Their flour is often used in regional dishes. Kabuli chickpeas, larger and lighter in color, are favored in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines, yielding a milder taste. Both types of chickpea powders are rich in protein and dietary fiber, catering to diverse culinary needs and health-conscious consumers.</p></p>
